The Economy of Pangasinan

The economies of Pangasinan are based on the agro-industrial and service sectors, with agriculture playing a key role in the northern part of the territory. The production of milkfish (bangus), rearing livestock, making a fish paste (bagoong), and other agro-industrial activities are the main drivers of the Pangasinan economy. However, there is no denying the value of trade, financial services, and educational services to the economy. Growing rice, tobacco, corn, sugarcane, and fruits, and raising pigs, chickens, goats, and carabaos are the main sources of income in the northern Ilocos provinces (water buffalos). Pangasinan has export earnings of around $5.5 million.


The national government's collection of tax income can be used to determine the distribution of economic activity in the area. The majority of the receipts—61% of the total—come from Pangasinan. The region's land transportation companies, such as Farinas Transit Company and Partas, as well as regional airlines, fuel the tourism economy, which concentrates on eco-tourism and coastal beaches. There are also plenty of opportunities to participate in other water sports and activities. Ecotourism makes use of the sea and forest resources in the area and showcases Region 1's natural beauty.

Energy

The 1200-megawatt Sual Coal-Fired Power Plant, 345-megawatt San Roque Multi-Purpose Dam, and the Northern Cement Corporation are all located in Pangasinan.

Marine

Pangasinan is a major fish supplier in Luzon as well as a major salt producer in the Philippines. Along the coasts of the Lingayen Gulf and the South China Sea, it has extensive fishponds, mostly for raising bangus, or "milkfish." Aquaculture in Pangasinan includes oysters and sea urchin farms.

Agriculture

Rice, mangoes, corn, and sugar cane are the main crops in Pangasinan. Pangasinan has a total land area of 536,819 hectares, with agricultural output accounting for 44 percent of the total land area.

Financial

Pangasinan has 593 banking and finance establishments.

Labor

Pangasinan has a labor force of roughly 1.52 million people, with 87 percent of them working.

Investment

The Philippines' Department of Trade and Industry has identified the following potential investment areas in Pangasinan:

  • Maguey production and handicraft center
  • Santiago Island Marine Park
  • Oyster processing facility
  • Bagoong technology and processing center
  • Tannery and leather production center
  • Oyster and aquaculture farming
  • Seaweed farming
  • Bamboo production
  • Handicraft and furniture making
  • Manufacture of construction bricks
  • Tourism development
